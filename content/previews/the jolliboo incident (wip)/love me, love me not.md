## where we left off
*some context before we begin*

So, picking up where we left off. It was an interesting cascade of decisions and influences all falling in the right moment. After a heart-wrenching process of letting go after I was let down slowly but also so quickly, I fell into a sort of depressive episode. Not exactly depression, but a sort of darkness grew inside. The failure, the pain, and the rejection.

Tolerating rejection wasn't always my strong suit. I never noticed it as just an incompatibility, but rather a failure. The loss of success. Something to be afraid of, or maybe ashamed of.

I don't know what happened to me in the first weeks of June. It was really bad that I started being unable to control myself at an emotional level, almost breaking from simple tasks and requests. I didn't feel like *me*. It felt like a different version of me, the version I was always trying to hide. That I was trying to control. I fell into that episode and luckily, my mother was there to comfort me when I really needed it.

I didn't know the consequences of such a harsh fall, a friendship that blossomed so much from the sixth star that it became a thorn in letting go. The attachment and the relationship involved all seemed like a risk worth taking, yet consequently breaking you apart once you had to let the ship sail into the far ocean.

Then, it started fading away. I stopped thinking about it. I tried to work, as much as I could. It took a month for those strong sparks to go away, now being only a lingering feeling. The voices in my head whispering "what if", no longer screaming it into my ear. But when those voices no longer had a host, it had to find a new one. Oh, how human nature has failed me.

## meet, jolly
*the new cast, our new muse*

Now, I'm not sure this new name *Jolly* would really fit the character I'm about to describe, but hang with me here. Jolly has been my friend (or more so, acquaintance) for the past year. They've been a great work partner, and they've been able to help me with loads of things especially in my own personal hobbies (and with their own curious projects too). I could list a whole things I appreciate about them, but this would turn into a love letter and I'm not making things any weirder.

I guess what really captured me was just the fact it felt like I was finally seen for once, not as someone who always kept looking and reaching out to feel a sense of warmth I materialised myself. No, it was someone who was actually *interested* in me, not in a way that was odd or romantic, but just a friend. We reciprocated our hobbies, thoughts, and philosophies. It was like a dream come true. It was so perfect and it felt *so* real.

It all clicked somewhere in the middle of June. I started seeing things I'd never seen before. Patterns in texting, random messages, and appreciation that I've just ignored. The coldness in my messages, a form of apathy due to my distance with them. I only saw them as a friend and a helper, but never as a lover. No, I never saw that as a potential.

But when I saw that someone was genuinely interested in me, I thought it might've been best to return the favour. It just clicked. Maybe all of this "they were interested in me" sort of thing was just a sort of delusional excuse that didn't cause such feelings to appear, but I guess I felt the friendship just grew on me. It grew warmer.

And as the days passed by, we kept talking. We kept playing. We kept laughing. I was so *in* love and I felt like I met a new best friend I could have. With the right time, in the right place. Everything felt so surreal.

Then, *everything* came back.
