# it's been a long *half*-year.
I'm not kidding. It's already been 6 months and a lot of things have happened. Not only was it because it was the start of this digital garden, but it was also because of the cascade of events that happened gradually over the months. Shall we start?

---
> This is only a recount and reflection of past events. The information presented here may not be the most accurate.
---
## january
January was a quiet month at first. Other than the beginning of the [cupstv](https://twitch.tv/cups_9) twitch channel, there wasn't really much. I discovered [Die With A Smile by Lady Gaga & Bruno Mars](https://open.spotify.com/track/2plbrEY59IikOBgBGLjaoe?si=a2ce091750f4404b) from my mum and it became one of my ultimate favourites.

However, there was one thing that kicked off all of these stressful events; my curiosity. I was curious and naive, trying to find answers to my faith. A faith that had a foundation, a simple one in fact. I sought out for answers to build it up, resorting to YouTube to find perspectives about this religion that I had been participating since my birth. 

I never looked at other peoples' perspectives on this matter, I always kept to myself. I wasn't a preacher, I was just a guy trying to live his life no matter where it took him. But that one month made me doubt everything. **I knew nothing.** I felt so much doubt that I even wrote [[My Essay on Christianity]] (later in February) simply due to the insanity it led in my mind. 

It wasn't easy. It felt like I was ripping myself apart, tearing my mind like it was mental torture. I felt voices in my head, conflicting and fighting. A "spiritual warfare" if you will. 

It really kicked off my future agnosticism, but I guess that's spoilers.

---

Then, there was my obsession with this someone (let's call them "volley"). *I was infatuated with the idea of them.* It became unhealthy, an admiration for someone who wouldn't even respond to me. I only met the person a few times in my life and I guess they were captivating, but not in terms of personality.

It was just a *crush*.

## february
I was back to school in February, and it was odd. I was going to be in Year 10 now, a crucial year in my school life due to academic requirements such as the [RoSA](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/leaving-school/rosa). I had to really focus this year and not let myself down. However, that wouldn't be so easy with some personal obstacles already in the way and the obstacles that were about to arrive right at my front doorstep.

My unhealthy obsession with Volley became reality. I thought I could reach out, but it hurt me instead. I remember risking my social security with this obsession of mine, with me buying a valentine's day rose and letter wanting to be *friends*. I even said "no strings attached". Oh, poor me. 

And then, there it was, I was ghosted into absolute nothing. Just like the past iterations. But that didn't hold me back. I was let down so quickly that it gave me whiplash, but it also gave me the motivation to do the work that I possibly could've. I started focusing on my work, not getting as many overdues as I usually did. I focused on solving my faith issue, consulting with friends and family for advice and help. Some gave me some really good advice and some gave me their own perspectives. It was a step in the right direction, even if it didn't give me the results I wanted.

It was a chaotic month, but it brought some growth in me. So, I'm very grateful that even though it hurt, it was a building block to my foundation as a person.

## march
March was more of an intermission period of me. At least, the early part of it. I was still confused and shaken by my faith *problem* and I was still quite hurt from Valentine's Day. I guess it all changed when I met someone else again during the end of March, not big sparks at first but just wanting to be friends. Let's call them, "starlight".

Starlight was a fascinating one, they were new to the school. I never really talked to them other than the casual "hello" and "hi", which I never usually did. Then, after a bit of time, we started to actually talk to each other.

I remember that there was an Industry Day event which helped students get some information about careers, as well as some advice from people who are already from certain companies. They described how careers were not just a straight-forward path and that there was a lot of change.

After carefully listening to what they had to say, I walked up to Starlight when the assembly was all over. We had a talk, some fascinating bits (e.g. I gave them a chocolate, which I got from a friend (my friend told me I could give it)), and then we walked up to break (or next period, I don't remember).

I remember the highlight of the day was that Starlight randomly, out of nowhere, asked me if I had a girlfriend. Of course, as a single teenage student who's a nerd at computers and does a lot of creative things, I said no (because I don't pull people like that). Still, it fascinated me.

It was a nice month, but I still felt so much chaos. I felt empty, but also full of thoughts I couldn't control properly.
